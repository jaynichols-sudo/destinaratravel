Europe This Summer: The Best Destinations for 2026

By Elizabeth Nichols May 2026 7 min read
← Back to Journal

Summer is when Europe truly comes alive — long golden evenings, open-air dinners, festivals in every piazza, and seas warm enough to swim. But it is also peak season, and the difference between a magical trip and a frustrating one comes down to where you go and how you time it. Elizabeth Nichols shares her favorite European destinations for summer 2026 — and exactly how to enjoy them without the crush of the crowds.

The Mediterranean Classics

The Amalfi Coast, Santorini & the French Riviera

There is a reason the Mediterranean coast defines European summer. The Amalfi Coast in June is pure magic — cliffside villages, lemon groves, and long lunches above turquoise water — and going early in the season means you beat the August Italian-holiday surge. Santorini and the Greek islands deliver that same warm-sea, white-village dream, while the French Riviera adds a dose of glamour from Nice to Saint-Tropez.

My advice for all three: base yourself slightly off the headline town. Stay in Praiano instead of Positano, in Antibes instead of Nice, or on a quieter Cycladic island a short ferry from Santorini. You get the same coastline and cuisine at a calmer pace, and you can day-trip into the famous spots early in the morning before the crowds and the midday heat arrive.

Mediterranean summer highlights
  • Amalfi Coast, Italy — cliffside villages and the Mediterranean at its most romantic
  • Santorini & the Cyclades, Greece — caldera sunsets and warm Aegean swimming
  • French Riviera — Nice, Antibes, and the glamour of the Cote d'Azur
  • Tip: stay one town over from the headliner to dodge the crowds

Northern Europe & the Midnight Sun

Iceland, the Norwegian Fjords & Scandinavia

If the Mediterranean feels too warm or too busy, summer is the only time the far north fully opens up — and it is spectacular. In Iceland, July brings near-endless daylight, accessible highland roads, and puffins on the cliffs. The Norwegian fjords are at their lushest, with waterfalls thundering down green walls straight into the sea, and the Scandinavian capitals — Copenhagen, Stockholm, Oslo — are at their most joyful, with locals living outdoors after the long winter.

The midnight sun is the secret weapon here: with daylight stretching past midnight, you can hike, cruise, or sightsee on a completely relaxed schedule. It is also a brilliant region for multi-generational travel and for travelers who simply do not do heat — comfortable temperatures, no crowds compared to the south, and scenery that genuinely stops you in your tracks.

Northern Europe summer highlights
  • Iceland — waterfalls, geysers, puffins, and 20+ hours of daylight
  • Norwegian Fjords — best explored by small ship in June and July
  • Scandinavia — Copenhagen, Stockholm, and Oslo at their liveliest
  • Tip: ideal for travelers who want cool weather and no crowds

Culture Without the Crowds

Tuscany, Paris & the Quiet Corners

Some of Europe's best summer experiences are found just inland from the headline destinations. Tuscany in summer is golden and unhurried — vineyard lunches, hilltop towns like Montepulciano and Siena, and warm evenings made for slow dinners. Paris, often abandoned by Parisians in August, takes on a calm, almost villagey feel, with shorter museum lines and riverside evenings along the Seine.

This is where a little local knowledge changes everything. Portugal's Douro Valley, Spain's Basque Country, Slovenia's Lake Bled, and Puglia in southern Italy all deliver world-class food and scenery with a fraction of the crowds. If 2026 is the year for a bucket-list European summer, consider pairing one famous destination with one of these quieter gems — it is the combination our clients rave about most.

Crowd-free summer picks
  • Tuscany, Italy — vineyards, hilltop towns, and long golden evenings
  • Paris in August — calmer, cooler lines, and a relaxed riverside city
  • Hidden gems — Douro Valley, Basque Country, Lake Bled, and Puglia
  • Tip: pair one icon with one quiet gem for the perfect balance

When & How to Book

Timing Your 2026 Summer Escape

European summer travel rewards planning ahead. The most desirable hotels and villas — especially on the Amalfi Coast, in Santorini, and in the Norwegian fjords — book up six to nine months out, and the best ones sell out first. If you have your heart set on a particular property or a specific week, the time to lock it in is now.

For the ideal balance of warm weather and manageable crowds, target the shoulder edges of summer: June and early July, or the first three weeks of September, when the sea is still warm but the August peak has passed. As your travel advisor, I can also unlock perks at many luxury properties — complimentary breakfast, upgrades, resort credits, and early check-in — that you simply cannot get booking on your own.

2026 summer booking tips
  • Book 6-9 months ahead for Amalfi, Santorini, and fjord properties
  • Target June, early July, or September for the best weather-to-crowd ratio
  • Ask about advisor perks — upgrades, breakfast, and resort credits
  • Build in a rest day; European summers are best savored, not sprinted
