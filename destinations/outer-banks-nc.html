Outer Banks, NC

Wild Shores & Timeless Coastal Charm

Stretching along the North Carolina coast, the Outer Banks is a land of raw natural beauty and storied American history. From wild horses roaming the beaches of Corolla to the towering sand dunes of Jockey's Ridge, this barrier island chain captivates visitors with its untamed landscape, legendary lighthouses, and laid-back coastal culture. Whether you're seeking adventure, history, or simply sun and sea, the Outer Banks delivers.

Destination Highlights

Cape Hatteras National Seashore

Explore miles of pristine, undeveloped Atlantic coastline under the protection of one of America's most beloved national seashores, perfect for surfing, fishing, and beachcombing.

Wild Horse Tours at Corolla

Witness the legendary wild Colonial Spanish Mustangs that roam the northern beaches — a thrilling encounter with living history on four-wheel-drive accessible shores.

Wright Brothers National Memorial

Stand on the very ground where Orville and Wilbur Wright made history's first powered flight in 1903 at Kill Devil Hills.

Jockey's Ridge Sand Dunes

Climb the tallest natural sand dune system on the East Coast for panoramic views of ocean and sound — and try hang gliding from its crest.

Ocracoke Island Ferry & Village

Take the scenic ferry to the charming, car-light village of Ocracoke, with its historic lighthouse, pirate heritage, and unspoiled beaches.

Fresh Seafood & Waterfront Dining

Savor the freshest local catch — from steamed blue crabs and hush puppies to shrimp po'boys and clam chowder — at waterfront restaurants across the Banks.
