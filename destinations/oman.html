Overview

The Hidden Gem of the Arabian Peninsula

Oman is the Arabian Peninsula's best-kept secret, a country of staggering natural beauty and authentic cultural experiences untouched by the rapid modernization of its neighbors. From the dramatic fjords of Musandam to the endless golden dunes of the Wahiba Sands, the landscape shifts from rugged mountains to pristine coastline.

The Sultanate has carefully preserved its rich heritage while welcoming visitors with genuine warmth. Magnificent forts and castles dot the landscape, ancient souks overflow with frankincense and silver, and traditional dhow boats still ply the turquoise waters along the coast.

Nature enthusiasts will find paradise here, whether tracking green sea turtles nesting on moonlit beaches, swimming in emerald wadi pools hidden in mountain gorges, spotting dolphins in the Gulf of Oman, or stargazing in some of the darkest skies in the Middle East.

Must-Do Activities

Top Experiences

Musandam Fjord Cruise

Sail through the stunning fjords of the Musandam Peninsula on a traditional dhow, spotting dolphins playing in the emerald waters framed by towering limestone cliffs.

Wahiba Sands Desert Camp

Journey into the rolling orange dunes of the Wahiba Sands for dune bashing, camel riding, and an unforgettable night under the stars at a luxury desert camp.

Wadi Shab Swimming

Hike through the spectacular Wadi Shab gorge, swimming through turquoise pools and scrambling over boulders to reach a hidden waterfall inside a cave.

Grand Mosque Visit

Marvel at the Sultan Qaboos Grand Mosque in Muscat, one of the most beautiful mosques in the world, featuring the second-largest hand-woven carpet and stunning crystal chandelier.

Sea Turtle Nesting

Witness the magical sight of endangered green sea turtles coming ashore to lay their eggs at Ras Al Jinz, one of the most important turtle nesting sites in the Indian Ocean.

Nizwa Fort and Souk

Explore the ancient capital of Nizwa with its impressive 17th-century fort and vibrant Friday cattle market, where Bedouin traders gather as they have for centuries.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

Recommended

Winter (Nov-Feb)

18-28°C

The best time to visit with comfortable temperatures ideal for outdoor exploration. Peak tourist season with pleasant desert camping weather and clear skies.

Recommended

Shoulder (Oct & Mar)

25-33°C

Warm but manageable temperatures with fewer crowds. Good hotel deals and still comfortable for morning and evening outdoor activities and desert excursions.

Spring (Apr-May)

30-38°C

Increasingly hot but the Salalah region starts its khareef monsoon preparations. Beach season continues along the coast. Lower prices at most accommodations.

Summer (Jun-Sep)

35-45°C

Extremely hot in most regions, but Salalah transforms into a green paradise during the khareef monsoon season from June to September, attracting regional visitors.

Helpful Advice

Travel Tips

🚗

Rent a 4x4

A 4x4 vehicle is essential for exploring Oman's best attractions. Many wadis, desert routes, and mountain roads require off-road capability. Book in advance during peak season.

👗

Dress Conservatively

Oman is traditional. Cover shoulders and knees in public. Women should carry a headscarf for mosque visits. Modest dress shows respect and is appreciated by locals.

⛽

Fuel Up Often

Gas stations can be sparse in remote areas. Fill your tank whenever you see a station, carry extra water, and share your itinerary with someone before venturing off the main roads.

🍵

Omani Hospitality

Omanis are incredibly welcoming. Accept offers of coffee and dates, which are a traditional gesture. Learn a few Arabic greetings to connect with locals on a deeper level.

💰

Cash is King

While major hotels accept cards, carry cash for souks, smaller restaurants, and rural areas. The Omani Rial is a strong currency so you will need less than you might expect.

🌊

Wadi Safety

Flash floods can occur in wadis even when skies are clear overhead. Never camp in a wadi bed, check weather forecasts, and avoid wadis during or after heavy rain in the mountains.
