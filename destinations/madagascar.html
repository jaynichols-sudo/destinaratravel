Madagascar

Overview

A World Apart

Madagascar is like nowhere else on Earth — an island so isolated for 88 million years that 90% of its wildlife exists nowhere else on the planet. From the eerie spiny forests of the south to the rainforests teeming with lemurs and chameleons, from the iconic Avenue of the Baobabs to pristine Indian Ocean beaches, Madagascar rewards adventurous travelers with experiences that redefine the meaning of unique.

Must-Do Activities

Top Experiences

Avenue of the Baobabs

Madagascar's most iconic image: ancient baobab trees lining a dirt road near Morondava at sunset.

Ranomafana National Park

Cloud forest refuge for golden bamboo lemurs, chameleons, and over 100 bird species.

Andasibe-Mantadia

The haunting call of the Indri lemur echoes through the eastern rainforest near Antananarivo.

Isalo National Park

Otherworldly sandstone massif with natural pools, lemur-filled canyons, and dramatic sunsets.

Île Sainte-Marie

Whale watching (July–Sept) when humpback whales gather to give birth in the warm bay.

Nosy Be

Madagascar's premier beach destination with diving, whale sharks, and relaxed island life.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

Dry Season (Apr–Nov)

Best overall for wildlife and road travel. July–September is ideal for whale watching at Île Sainte-Marie.

Hot & Rainy (Dec–Mar)

Cyclone risk on the east coast. Some roads impassable. Lush greenery but difficult travel conditions.

Shoulder (Apr & Nov)

Sweet spots — end/start of dry season with fewer tourists and competitive prices.

Whale Season (Jul–Sep)

Humpback whales congregate at Île Sainte-Marie in spectacular numbers. Book well in advance.

Your Journey

A Sample 7-Day Itinerary

1

Arrival in Antananarivo

Arrive in the highland capital and settle into a refined retreat before your island adventure unfolds.

2

Andasibe Rainforest

Journey east to a luxury lodge, then search the rainforest for the indri and the haunting call of lemurs at dusk.

3

Andasibe Exploration

A private guided trek reveals chameleons, orchids, and elusive nocturnal wildlife on an after-dark walk.

4

Fly to the Avenue of the Baobabs

Travel west to witness the towering baobabs glow at sunset along their celebrated avenue.

5

Fly to Nosy Be

Transfer to the island of Nosy Be and unwind at a beachfront sanctuary above the Indian Ocean.

6

Private Island & Reef

Sail to a secluded islet for snorkeling among vivid reefs and a barefoot lunch on powder-white sand.

7

Departure

A final morning by the sea before your transfer onward, Madagascar's wild beauty lingering.

Practical Advice

Travel Tips

🧭

Fly Domestically

Madagascar's roads are challenging — domestic flights with Air Madagascar save days of bumpy travel.

🌍

Malaria Precautions

Malaria is present in lowland and coastal areas. Take prophylactics and use DEET insect repellent.

💡

Hire Local Guides

Required in national parks and essential for wildlife spotting. Local guides have extraordinary eyes for camouflaged animals.

🧳

Cash is King

ATMs are unreliable outside Antananarivo. Carry sufficient euros or USD to exchange.
