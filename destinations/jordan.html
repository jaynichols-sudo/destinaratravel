Jordan

Overview

Petra, the Dead Sea & Desert Wonders

Jordan is a land of extraordinary contrasts — where rose-red Petra rises from desert canyons, Bedouin traditions thrive alongside modern cities, and the therapeutic waters of the Dead Sea draw visitors from around the world. From the mystical Wadi Rum to the Crusader castles of the north, Jordan offers an immersive journey through millennia of civilization.

Must-Do Activities

Top Experiences

Petra

Walk the Siq canyon to reach the iconic Treasury at sunrise in the rose-red Nabataean city.

Wadi Rum

Lawrence of Arabia's desert: vast red sandstone valleys, Bedouin camps, and starlit skies.

Dead Sea

Float effortlessly in the world's saltiest lake; mineral-rich mud is a natural spa treatment.

Jerash

One of the best-preserved Roman provincial cities on earth with grand colonnaded streets.

Aqaba

Jordan's Red Sea coast: world-class snorkeling and diving in warm, crystal-clear waters.

Dana Biosphere Reserve

Spectacular hiking through Jordan's largest nature reserve spanning four eco-zones.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

Spring (Mar–May)

Ideal temperatures 15–25°C, wildflowers in bloom, and lighter crowds. Perfect for hiking Petra and Dana Reserve.

Summer (Jun–Aug)

Very hot (35–40°C in Wadi Rum). Manageable with early morning starts; Dead Sea resorts are popular.

Autumn (Sep–Nov)

Excellent conditions return — warm days, cool evenings, and great for outdoor adventures.

Winter (Dec–Feb)

Cool and sometimes rainy. Amman can get snow. Petra is magical and nearly crowd-free.

Your Journey

A Sample 7-Day Itinerary

1

Arrival in Amman

Ease into the capital with a private dinner and a first taste of Jordanian hospitality at your boutique hotel.

2

Jerash & the Citadel

Walk the colonnaded streets of Roman Jerash, then survey Amman from its ancient hilltop citadel.

3

Dead Sea

Float effortlessly upon the mineral waters and surrender to a private spa ritual at the world's lowest shore.

4

Petra

Pass through the Siq to the Treasury's first golden reveal, exploring the rose-red city with your private archaeologist.

5

Petra by Candlelight

Discover the high places of Petra by day, then return for the hushed magic of the city lit by a thousand candles.

6

Wadi Rum

Traverse crimson dunes by private vehicle, then settle into a luxury desert camp beneath Wadi Rum's vast sky.

7

Departure

A final desert sunrise before your seamless transfer onward, the silence of Wadi Rum lingering.

Practical Advice

Travel Tips

🧭

Buy the Jordan Pass

It covers your visa fee + entry to Petra and 40+ attractions. Buy online before you arrive.

🌍

Dress Modestly

Cover shoulders and knees when visiting mosques and conservative areas. Lightweight layers work perfectly.

💡

Ramadan Timing

If visiting during Ramadan, be respectful of fasting hours. Many restaurants close during the day but evenings are festive.

🧳

Stay Hydrated

Jordan's desert climate is intense — carry at least 2 liters of water daily when touring Petra and Wadi Rum.
