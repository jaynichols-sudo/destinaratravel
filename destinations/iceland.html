Iceland

Land of Fire & Ice — Northern Lights & Volcanic Wonders

Iceland is one of the world's most dramatic landscapes — a young volcanic island where geysers erupt, glaciers calve into lagoons, waterfalls cascade from ancient lava fields, and the Northern Lights dance overhead on winter nights. In summer, the midnight sun bathes endless landscapes in golden light. Iceland rewards every traveler with an intensity of natural experience rarely found anywhere on earth.

Destination Highlights

Northern Lights — Aurora Borealis

Chase the Aurora Borealis on winter nights across Iceland's dark skies — from Þingvellir to the remote Westfjords — one of the most magical natural spectacles on earth.

Golden Circle — Geysers & Waterfalls

Drive the iconic Golden Circle: Þingvellir National Park where tectonic plates meet, the erupting Geysir hot spring field, and the thundering 32-meter Gullfoss waterfall.

Blue Lagoon Geothermal Spa

Soak in Iceland's iconic milky-blue geothermal waters rich in silica and minerals — surrounded by ancient lava fields and steam, open year-round even on snowy winter days.

Jökulsárlón Glacier Lagoon

Float among massive blue-white icebergs in Jökulsárlón Glacier Lagoon, then watch them wash ashore on Diamond Beach as waves crash over glittering natural sculptures.

Midnight Sun & Puffins

Experience the extraordinary midnight sun in summer — hike at 11pm in golden daylight, photograph nesting puffins on sea cliffs, and drive scenic highland roads under amber skies.

Waterfalls, Lava Caves & Volcanic Beaches

Explore Skógafoss and Seljalandsfoss waterfalls, the eerie Thórsmörk lava landscape, the black sand Reynisfjara beach, and the South Coast's extraordinary raw volcanic scenery.
