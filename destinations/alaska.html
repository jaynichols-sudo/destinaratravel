Overview

America's Last Frontier

Alaska is a land of superlatives: the largest state, the tallest peak in North America, the longest coastline, and some of the most dramatic wilderness on Earth. From the massive glaciers of Glacier Bay to the towering peaks of Denali, Alaska offers experiences that cannot be found anywhere else.

Whether you dream of watching grizzly bears catch salmon at Katmai, cruising through the Inside Passage on a luxury ship, witnessing the ethereal northern lights, or fishing for king salmon in crystal-clear rivers, Alaska delivers on every front.

At Destinara, we craft Alaska adventures that combine the thrill of wilderness with the comfort of luxury lodges and world-class cruise lines, ensuring every moment is extraordinary.

Top Experiences
Unforgettable Alaska Moments
From glacier trekking to wildlife encounters, Alaska offers once-in-a-lifetime experiences at every turn.

Glacier Exploration

Witness the awe-inspiring power of tidewater glaciers calving into the sea. Kayak among icebergs in Glacier Bay, hike on the Matanuska Glacier, or take a helicopter tour over the vast Juneau Icefield.

Wildlife Watching

Alaska is one of the best places on Earth for wildlife. Watch grizzly bears fish for salmon at Katmai, spot humpback whales breaching in the Inside Passage, and see bald eagles soaring overhead.

Northern Lights

From late August through April, the aurora borealis paints the Alaskan sky with mesmerizing curtains of green, purple, and pink. Fairbanks offers some of the best viewing in the world.

Inside Passage Cruises

Sail through the stunning Inside Passage with cruise lines like Viking, Celebrity, and Royal Caribbean. Visit charming ports like Juneau, Skagway, and Ketchikan surrounded by breathtaking scenery.

Denali National Park

Home to North America's tallest peak at 20,310 feet, Denali offers unparalleled wilderness. Take a flightseeing tour, go backcountry hiking, or ride the park bus deep into grizzly country.

Salmon Fishing

Cast your line for world-class king salmon, sockeye, and silver salmon in pristine Alaskan rivers. Whether seasoned angler or first-timer, guided fishing trips offer unforgettable catches.

Travel Tips
Know Before You Go
Insider tips from our Alaska travel experts to help you plan the perfect frontier adventure.
🌪️

Layer Your Clothing

Alaska weather is unpredictable. Pack layers including a waterproof shell, fleece, and moisture-wicking base layers. Even in summer, temperatures vary dramatically throughout the day.

📷

Book Excursions Early

Popular experiences like bear-viewing flights, helicopter glacier treks, and fishing charters sell out months in advance. Book 6–9 months ahead for summer travel.

🚢

Cruise + Land Package

Combine an Inside Passage cruise with a land tour to Denali and Fairbanks for the complete Alaska experience. This is the best way to see the most in a single trip.

🛠️

Bug Spray is Essential

Mosquitoes can be intense in summer, especially inland. Pack DEET-based insect repellent and consider a head net for backcountry excursions.

🌞

Embrace the Midnight Sun

In summer, Alaska gets up to 22 hours of daylight. Bring a sleep mask and take advantage of the extended hours for hiking, fishing, and wildlife watching.

💉

Travel Insurance is a Must

Alaska's remote locations mean weather delays can happen. Comprehensive travel insurance ensures you are covered for any disruptions to your itinerary.
