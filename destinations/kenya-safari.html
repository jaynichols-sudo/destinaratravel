Overview

Where the Wild Things Are

Kenya is the birthplace of the safari experience and remains the gold standard for African wildlife encounters. The vast plains of the Masai Mara, the flamingo-lined shores of Lake Nakuru, and the snow-capped peak of Mount Kenya create landscapes that define the African continent.

Every year, millions of wildebeest and zebra thunder across the Mara River in the Great Migration, one of nature most spectacular events. Year-round, Kenya offers incredible opportunities to see lions, elephants, leopards, rhinos, and buffalo in their natural habitat.

Beyond wildlife, Kenya rich cultural heritage shines through encounters with the Maasai people, whose traditional lifestyle and vibrant ceremonies offer a profound connection to the land they have called home for centuries.

Must-Do Activities

Top Experiences

Great Migration

Witness millions of wildebeest cross the Mara River in a dramatic spectacle of survival, accompanied by zebra and gazelle on their annual journey.

Big Five Game Drive

Track lions, elephants, buffalo, leopards, and rhinos on guided game drives through the Masai Mara, Amboseli, or Tsavo national parks.

Balloon Safari

Float silently over the Masai Mara at dawn in a hot air balloon, watching the savanna come alive below as the sun paints the landscape golden.

Maasai Village Visit

Experience the traditional culture of the Maasai people, learning about their customs, dances, and deep connection to the land and wildlife.

Mount Kenya Trek

Challenge yourself with a trek up Africa second-highest peak, passing through diverse ecosystems from tropical forest to alpine moorland.

Diani Beach Coast

Relax on stunning white sand beaches of the Kenyan coast, with world-class snorkeling, dhow sailing, and Swahili cultural experiences.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

Migration (Jul-Oct)

72F

The Great Migration reaches the Masai Mara with dramatic river crossings. Dry weather makes wildlife viewing excellent across all parks.

Dry (Jan-Feb)

79F

Another excellent dry season with great visibility. Calving season with abundant predator activity and newborn animals everywhere.

Shoulder (Jun, Nov)

68F

Fewer tourists and good wildlife viewing. June sees migration beginning and November brings short rains that refresh the landscape.

Wet (Mar-May)

75F

Long rains make some roads impassable but the landscape is lush and green. Lowest prices and excellent bird watching.

Practical Advice

Travel Tips

📷

Bring Good Optics

A quality camera with a telephoto lens and binoculars are essential. Wildlife can be distant and the best shots require a zoom of at least 200mm.

🧥

Layer Your Clothing

Early morning game drives can be cold. Pack warm layers, a hat, and sunscreen. Neutral colors like khaki and green are best for safari.

💊

Malaria Precautions

Consult your doctor about antimalarial medication before travel. Use insect repellent and sleep under mosquito nets in lowland areas.

🚙

Book a Good Guide

An experienced safari guide makes all the difference. They know animal behavior, the best viewing spots, and share fascinating ecosystem insights.

🔋

Charge Everything

Power can be limited at safari camps. Bring extra batteries and a portable power bank. Most lodges charge devices overnight.

🌍

Support Conservation

Choose eco-friendly lodges and conservancies that give back to local communities and wildlife protection efforts across Kenya.
