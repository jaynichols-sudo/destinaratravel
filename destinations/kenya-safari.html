Kenya Safari

Africa's Greatest Wildlife Spectacle

Home›Destinations›Kenya Safari

Kenya is the gold standard for African safaris — a land where the Great Migration thunders across the Maasai Mara, where Big Five sightings are almost guaranteed, and where Maasai warriors still guard their ancient traditions. From the snow-capped peak of Mount Kenya to the white sand beaches of the coast, this is a country of breathtaking diversity and profound natural beauty.

Highlights & Experiences

✦🦁 Maasai Mara — Witness the Great Migration (July–Oct) and year-round Big Five safaris in Kenya's iconic reserve.
✦🐘 Amboseli — Iconic elephant herds against the backdrop of Mount Kilimanjaro across the Tanzanian border.
✦🦒 Samburu — Rare species found only here: Grevy's zebra, reticulated giraffe, and Somali ostrich.
✦🌋 Nakuru & Naivasha — Flamingo-lined lakes in the Rift Valley with boat rides and Hell's Gate hiking.
✦🏔️ Mount Kenya — Africa's second-highest peak with trekking through moorland and alpine glaciers.
✦🏖️ Diani Beach — Pristine Indian Ocean coast near Mombasa with turquoise water and Swahili culture.

Why Kenya Safari?

  • Witness the Greatest Wildlife Spectacle on Earth — the Great Migration
  • Nearly guaranteed Big Five sightings across multiple national parks
  • World-class luxury tented camps and eco-lodges
  • Authentic Maasai culture and community tourism experiences
  • Combine bush and beach for an extraordinary vacation
  • Outstanding birding with over 1,100 recorded species
  • Excellent value compared to Tanzania for similar safari quality
  • Professional English-speaking guides with deep ecological knowledge

Your 5-Day Itinerary

Day 1

Nairobi Arrival & City Highlights

  • Arrive Nairobi — check into a Westlands or Karen neighborhood hotel
  • Visit the Giraffe Centre to hand-feed endangered Rothschild giraffes
  • Tour the David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust elephant orphanage (morning only)
  • Dinner at Carnivore restaurant — a Nairobi institution
Day 2

Fly to Maasai Mara

  • Morning flight to the Mara (45 minutes from Wilson Airport)
  • Check into luxury tented camp overlooking the plains
  • Afternoon game drive — lions, cheetahs, and wildebeest await
  • Sundowner drinks on the savannah; bush dinner under the stars
Day 3

Full Day Maasai Mara

  • Dawn game drive for predator activity at first light
  • Return for breakfast; rest during the hottest midday hours
  • Afternoon drive to the Mara River — July–Oct: witness wildebeest crossings
  • Evening lecture with your guide about Maasai culture and ecology
Day 4

Amboseli National Park

  • Morning flight or road transfer to Amboseli (4 hrs by road)
  • Afternoon game drive — Amboseli's large elephant herds are legendary
  • Photograph elephants with Kilimanjaro as the backdrop at dawn/dusk
  • Cultural visit to a Maasai village for traditional welcome ceremony
Day 5

Final Safari & Departure

  • Final sunrise game drive in Amboseli
  • Transfer to Nairobi for afternoon flight or coastal extension to Diani Beach
  • Depart with memories that will last a lifetime
  • Optional: Add 3–4 nights at Diani Beach on the Indian Ocean coast

Insider Travel Tips

📅

Book Camps Early

The best Mara camps sell out 12–18 months in advance for July–October migration season. Don't delay.

🟢

Pack Neutral Colors

Khaki, green, and brown blend into the bush. Avoid white and bright colors that can disturb wildlife.

💊

Malaria Prevention

Consult your doctor 4–6 weeks before travel. Most safari areas require malaria prophylaxis.

📷

Bring Binoculars

A good pair of binoculars (8x42 or 10x42) rivals a zoom lens for spotting distant wildlife.

When to Visit

🦓

Great Migration (Jul–Oct)

Peak season — the Mara is teeming with wildlife. Book 12+ months ahead. July–August sees dramatic river crossings.

🌧️

Long Rains (Apr–Jun)

Green season — lush landscapes, newborn animals, excellent birdwatching. Fewer crowds, lower rates.

☀️

Dry Season (Jan–Mar)

Excellent game viewing as animals gather at water sources. The Mara is at its most accessible.

🌦️

Short Rains (Nov–Dec)

Brief afternoon showers keep it green. Migratory birds arrive. Good value with fewer tourists.
