Nashville, TN

Overview

Music City & Southern Hospitality

Nashville is much more than the home of country music — it's a thriving, creative city that has emerged as one of America's most exciting destinations. From the legendary honky-tonks of Lower Broadway to the world's finest hot chicken, from vibrant street art and world-class museums to a culinary scene that draws chefs from across the country, Music City delivers non-stop energy and Southern hospitality in equal measure.

Must-Do Activities

Top Experiences

Lower Broadway

The beating heart of Nashville: honky-tonks, live music, and neon lights from dusk to midnight.

Country Music Hall of Fame

The world's largest country music research center and museum.

Ryman Auditorium

The "Mother Church of Country Music" — every performance here is legendary.

Hot Chicken

Nashville's iconic spicy fried chicken at Hattie B's, Prince's, or Bolton's.

The Parthenon

A full-scale replica of the Athens Parthenon stands in Centennial Park.

The Gulch & 12South

Trendy neighborhoods filled with street murals, boutiques, and acclaimed restaurants.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

Spring (Apr–May)

Perfect weather, CMA Fest prep, and blooming Centennial Park. The best time to visit overall.

Summer (Jun–Aug)

Hot and humid (90°F+) but festival season. CMA Fest in June draws 80,000+ fans to the city.

Fall (Sep–Nov)

Excellent weather returns. Football season energizes the city. Great restaurant reservations and hotel rates.

Winter (Dec–Feb)

Mild by southern standards. Holiday events, fewer tourists, and easy reservations. Occasional ice storms.

Your Journey

A Sample 7-Day Itinerary

1

Arrival in Nashville

Settle into your stylish downtown hotel, then enjoy a welcome dinner and an intimate evening of live music.

2

Music City Heritage

A private tour of legendary studios and the city's storied music landmarks, with premium evening seats.

3

Culinary & Distilleries

A guided tasting tour of Southern kitchens and Tennessee whiskey, capped by a refined chef's dinner.

4

Belle Meade & Estates

A chauffeured visit to historic estates and gardens, followed by an elegant afternoon in the city's salons.

5

Franklin & Countryside

A scenic drive to charming Franklin for antiquing and a long, unhurried country luncheon.

6

Arts & Sunset

Explore Nashville's galleries and design districts, then sunset cocktails from a coveted rooftop terrace.

7

Leisurely Departure

A final Southern brunch and last note of live music before your private transfer to the airport.

Practical Advice

Travel Tips

🧭

Book Opry Tickets Early

Grand Ole Opry tickets sell out weeks in advance, especially on weekend nights. Book as soon as your dates are set.

🌍

Rent a Car for Day Trips

Nashville's city center is walkable, but a car is needed for distillery tours and surrounding attractions.

💡

Start Broadway Late

The honky-tonks are still going strong at midnight. Pace yourself — Nashville rewards night owls.

🧳

Hot Chicken Warning

Nashville hot chicken is genuinely very hot. Start with "Medium" level on your first visit — not "Shut the Cluck Up."
