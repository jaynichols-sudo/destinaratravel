Nuremberg Christmas Market

Bavaria's Most Beloved Yuletide Tradition

Every year since the 16th century, Nuremberg's medieval old town has transformed into one of the world's most enchanting Christmas markets. The Christkindlesmarkt — presided over by the golden-robed Christkind — fills the main square with the scent of gingerbread, roasted nuts, and mulled wine. Framed by Gothic architecture and twinkling lights, it is the quintessential German Christmas experience.

Destination Highlights

Christkindlesmarkt Main Square

Browse over 180 stalls in the historic Hauptmarkt, selling handcrafted ornaments, wooden toys, and traditional Franconian treats beneath a glowing golden backdrop.

Nuremberg Gingerbread (Lebkuchen)

Sample the world-famous Nuremberg Lebkuchen — soft, spiced gingerbread that has been a local specialty since the 14th century, sold in beautifully decorated tins.

Imperial Castle Tour

Explore the impressive Nuremberg Imperial Castle perched above the old town, offering sweeping views over the illuminated medieval cityscape below.

Old Town Medieval Walks

Wander cobblestone lanes past half-timbered houses, Gothic churches, and ornate fountains that have witnessed five centuries of Christmas tradition.

Traditional Bratwurst & Glühwein

Warm up with Nuremberg's iconic finger-sized Rostbratwürste served in a bread roll, paired with a steaming mug of fragrant mulled wine (Glühwein).

Children's Railway & Nativity Scene

Delight in the magical children's section of the market, featuring a miniature railway, a vast nativity display, and a dedicated children's Christmas market.
