Tuscany, Italy

Rolling Hills, Renaissance Art & Timeless Flavors

Tuscany is the Italy of the imagination — rolling hills blanketed in cypress trees and vineyards, medieval hilltop towns, world-class Renaissance art, and an extraordinary food and wine culture. From the cobblestone streets of Florence to the thermal springs of Saturnia and the wine estates of Chianti, Tuscany offers a richness of experience that rewards every traveler who wanders its timeless landscape.

Destination Highlights

Florence Renaissance Art & Architecture

Explore the birthplace of the Renaissance — home to Michelangelo's David, Botticelli's Primavera in the Uffizi, and Brunelleschi's magnificent Cathedral dome.

Chianti Wine Country Tours

Drive the Via Chiantigiana through vineyard-covered hills, stopping at historic estates for barrel tastings of world-renowned Chianti Classico wines.

Siena Medieval Old Town

Wander the perfectly preserved medieval city of Siena, centered on the stunning Piazza del Campo — one of Europe's greatest medieval squares.

San Gimignano's Medieval Towers

Visit the "Manhattan of the Middle Ages" — a hilltop town bristling with ancient stone towers, unchanged since the 13th century.

Val d'Orcia Scenic Drives

Drive through the UNESCO-listed Val d'Orcia — the quintessential Tuscan landscape of rolling golden fields, lone cypress trees, and ancient farmhouses.

Truffle Hunting & Farm-to-Table Dining

Join a traditional truffle hunt in the forests of San Miniato, then enjoy farm-to-table meals featuring handmade pasta, local olive oil, and seasonal produce.
