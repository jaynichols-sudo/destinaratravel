Overview

A Tapestry of Ancient Wonders and Modern Vibrancy

Mexico is a destination that captivates the imagination with its remarkable blend of pre-Columbian heritage, colonial splendor, and contemporary creativity. From the towering pyramids of Teotihuacan to the colorful streets of Oaxaca, every corner of this vast nation tells a story spanning thousands of years of human civilization.

The country's natural landscapes are equally breathtaking, encompassing Caribbean coastlines with crystal-clear cenotes, rugged Sierra Madre mountain ranges, lush tropical jungles teeming with wildlife, and arid deserts that stretch to the horizon. Each region offers its own distinct character and unforgettable experiences.

Mexican cuisine, recognized by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage, is a journey in itself. From street-side tacos and mole sauces with dozens of ingredients to mezcal tastings in Oaxaca and fresh ceviche on the coast, the flavors of Mexico reflect the soul of its warm and welcoming people.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

Mexico's climate varies by region, but the dry season is generally ideal for most destinations.
Recommended

Nov - February

Peak dry season with comfortable temperatures. Ideal for beaches and cultural sites. Whale watching season in Baja.

Recommended

March - April

Warm and dry with fewer crowds. Perfect for archaeological sites and spring festivals. Great for cenote swimming.

May - June

Beginning of rainy season with afternoon showers. Lower prices and thinner crowds at popular destinations.

July - October

Rainy season with humidity and occasional hurricanes on the coast. Lush green landscapes and the best hotel deals.

Practical Advice

Travel Tips

💧

Drink Bottled Water

Always drink purified or bottled water. Most restaurants use filtered water and ice, but it is best to confirm first.

🚌

Use ADO Buses

First-class ADO buses are comfortable and affordable for traveling between cities. Book online for the best seats and prices.

💬

Learn Basic Spanish

While tourist areas have English speakers, knowing basic Spanish greatly enhances your experience in local markets and towns.

💰

Carry Pesos for Markets

While tourist areas accept US dollars, you will get much better prices paying in Mexican pesos at markets and local shops.

💉

Travel Insurance Recommended

We recommend comprehensive travel insurance for Mexico. Ensure it covers medical expenses and adventure activities like diving.

🌞

Arrive Early at Ruins

Visit archaeological sites early morning to avoid crowds and midday heat. Chichen Itza and Teotihuacan get very busy by 10am.
